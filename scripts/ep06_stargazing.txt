Come outside.

Step quietly through the door and let it close behind you.

Take a slow breath in.
The night air is cool and clean.
And out.
Your shoulders drop a little.

You are standing in an open field.
The grass is soft under your feet.
The air carries the smell of earth and sky.
And above you, the stars.

So many stars.
More than you might expect.
More than you could count in a lifetime.

Take a moment to just look upward.
Let the scale of it settle over you.
Not in a way that feels overwhelming.
In a way that feels relieving.

You are a small, warm, breathing thing beneath an enormous and ancient sky.
And somehow that is deeply comforting.

There is a blanket on the grass.
A reclining chair, or perhaps just a soft mat.
Whatever you need.
You lie back.

You feel the ground beneath you.
Solid.
Steady.
Reliable.
The earth has been here for four and a half billion years.
It is not going anywhere.
And for now, neither are you.

Let your body soften into it.

Begin at the crown of your head.
Let your scalp loosen.
Let your forehead smooth.
Let the crease between your brows release.

Soften around your eyes.
Let your eyes rest beneath closed lids.
No flickering.
No scanning.
Just still.

Let your cheeks relax.
Let your jaw fall open the smallest amount.
Let your lips rest apart.

The cool air touches your face.
A gentle breath of night.
Quiet and clean.

Your neck releases.
The muscles that have held your head up all day, they can rest.
You are supported by the earth.

Your shoulders let go.
Down.
And further down.
Heavy and open.

Your arms rest on the blanket.
Your hands are open.
Palms facing the sky.
As if you are receiving the starlight.

Your chest rises and falls.
Slow and even.
The rhythm of rest.

Your belly softens.
Your hips sink.
Your legs grow heavy.
Your feet rest.

You are completely here.
Completely held.
Completely still.

Now look up, in your mind's eye.

The stars are various.
Some white, some faintly blue, some with the barest hint of gold.
Some are close in clusters.
Some are lone and distant.

The Milky Way moves across the sky in a soft band.
Not quite a cloud.
Not quite a river.
Something between.
Something older than language.

You do not need to name any of it.
You do not need to understand any of it.
You can simply look, and breathe, and be beneath it.

Let your breathing be slow and full.
Each inhale drawing in the coolness of the night.
Each exhale releasing the warmth of the day.

If your thoughts are moving, that is fine.
Let them move.
But give them less and less of your attention.
The sky is more interesting.
The breath is more immediate.
The stillness is more useful.

With each number, feel the sky growing wider above you.
Feel yourself growing softer beneath it.

Imagine one particular star.
Perhaps the brightest one you can see.
It is very far away.
The light you are seeing left that star long ago.
Maybe hundreds of years.
Maybe thousands.

It has been traveling through empty space all that time, just to arrive here, just now, at your eye.

And you received it.
Without trying.
Without doing anything at all.

That is enough.
You are enough.
Simply being here, receiving, resting.
That is enough.

The night deepens.
The temperature drops just a little more.
The blanket feels warmer in contrast.
The stars seem sharper.

Somewhere in the field, crickets sing.
A low, rhythmic sound.
Rising and falling.
An ancient lullaby.
Old enough to have sung other animals to sleep long before humans arrived to listen.

Let it do its work now.
Let the rhythm of the crickets and the rhythm of your breathing find each other.
Let them become one quiet sound.

In.
And out.
In.
And out.

The earth beneath you is warm.
Even in the night, it holds the warmth of the day.
It passes that warmth to you.
Through the blanket.
Through your back.
Into your body.

You are being held.
Warmed.
Sustained.

A meteor moves across the sky.
A brief, bright line.
And then it is gone.
And the sky is unchanged.
Still full.
Still calm.
Still vast.

Any thought that is troubling you can be like that meteor.
Let it move across.
Let it burn out quickly.
Let the sky remain unchanged and full when it is gone.
